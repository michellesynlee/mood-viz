my 2026 mood tracker

i have been tracking my mood every day since the start of 2026. i realized that there were patterns in my mood shifts, and i wanted to visualize them in a way that was more fun than words.

i found upon reflection that to me, emotions were like the ocean. they bubbled below the surface and had a world of their own that could not be seen from the outside, but affected the climate of my life. like the ocean, my emotions also had layers. they were a life of their own, and were always changing.

scroll down to travel underwater and explore my emotional world. there are new visualizations each month—some made with D3, and some made with hand-drawn art.

january

i found january to quite literally have the ups and downs of waves. like clockwork, every 2-4 days i found my mood shifting from a high to a low, and then back up again.

the height of the wave represents how good my day was on a scale of 1-10, with 10 being the highest. the color of the dots represents my anxiety level that day, with darker colors representing lower anxiety, as i associate dark colors with feeling more grounded and less transient.

hover over the dots on the waves to see more detail.

february

i noticed that my emotions in feburary did not follow much of a pattern: they just came and went without any direction or warning, and that became my inspiration for creating a visualization out of floating jellyfish. i am not the best artist, and while this method is less accurate, i wanted to explore this creative method.

i drew 4 jellyfish, each representing a week in feburary (left to right), and tentacles representing the days of the week. the width, length, and shade of each tentacle conveys different information about that day.
the length of the tentacle represents how good my overall day was, rated from 1-10. the max length, which is a 10, is represented by the gray mouth in the middle of each jellyfish.
the width of the tentacle represents my anxiety level that day from 1-10, with 10 being the widest. max width is also represented by the gray mouth in the middle. i chose the width to represent my anxiety level because when my anxiety is high, it feels like it is taking up more space in my mind and life.

finally, the shade of the tentacle represents how in control of my emotions i felt that day. the darker the shade, the more i felt in control, and the lighter the shade, the less. i chose color for this variable because when i dont feel in control of my emotions, i feel less like myself, and with that, i feel less saturated.
